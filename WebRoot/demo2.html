What do the lights (LEDs) mean?

  • Power on: Red and green lights will alternate blinks after holding button down for 1 second.
  • Charging: Red light will blink every 1 second.
  • Charging Complete: Green light will be indicated and remain on.
  • Searching for Device: 2 red lights will blink every 1 second.
  • Works Properly: Green light will blink every 1 second to indicate device is working.
  • Low Battery: Red light will be indicated and remain on.

GPS, LBS and WiFi Scan, which one is best? Can they be used together or have to be one at a time?

  • LBS: This refers to the Location Based Services. When a device is outside of the Wifi scan range, LBS will be activated to locate your device through cell towers.
  • GPS: If you canont connect to LBS, GPS will activate if connected to a cellular network. It will not turn on without a cellular network, and only during real time tracking should it be turned on. GPS location signals are sent from satellites in orbit.
  • Real time tracking: A device can be located using real time tracking. This occurs when the Wi-Fi scan, BS and GPS are all turned on. This allows for a faster and more accurate location discovery.

What Affects GPS Signal?

  • The GPS signal and accuracy of your Paby is affected various factors including weather, obstructions and metal objects. Here are a few ways to improve the GPS signal strength and accuracy of your Paby:
  • Clear view of the sky (no obstructions from building and trees which could block the signal)
  • Prevent interference from metal objects as it will affect the signal strength. This includes using the Paby inside of a car or shed.
  • Keep your battery charged for optimal performance.
  • When you're not on your Home Wi-Fi network, the Paby will first scan for other familiar Wi-Fi networks for a location before it searches for a GPS signal. However, a GPS is generally more accurate.
  • Urban areas
    • There are a number of WiFi points available (from surrounding houses) to help the Paby triangulate a position without GPS.
    • Possible limitations on GPS signal strength due to building obstructions
    • Cell reception generally strong
  • Rural areas
    • There are a limited number of WiFi points available (given the reduction in property density) to triangulate a position
    • Clear and open areas make it beneficial to obtain a strong GPS signal, however is still subject to cloud coverage
    • Possible poor cell reception out in rural areas. Please check the coverage in your area.

What affects cellular reception?

In order to make sure your area has coverage, check out the 3G/2G coverage in your area. If you do have coverage, here are a few other factors that may affect the cell signal strength of your Paby:

  • Elevation - deep within a valley means a weaker signal strength
  • Interference - manmade or natural obstructions such as buildings or trees can interfere with the signal strength
  • Lack of towers (more prominent in rural areas) - with an increase in distance to the tower comes a weaker signal strength

How do I check to see if my Home WiFi is set up correctly?

  • You can easily check to see if your Home WiFi configuration was successful by looking for the indicated WiFi at the top of the APP, located next to the battery symbol. If you see the WiFi bars indicated in green, this means you are connected to your WiFi.
